Facts & Demographics

- Geography

Bohol is located in the Central Visayas Region (Region VII) and consists of 75 islands, spanning 4,821 km². The province has 1,109 barangays, 47 municipalities, and one component city, its capital Tagbilaran City.

- Population

Population: 1,412,726

Interesting Facts you probably didn't know!

- Chocolate Hills are uplifted coral reefs:

The Chocolate Hills are one of Bohol's famous landmarks. An interesting fact about these hills is that they were formed from the fossilization of ancient coral reefs. After hundreds of years, a tectonic uplift pushed the reefs upward. Erosion then formed the symmetrical hills that we know of today.

- Danajon Bank is one of only six double barrier reefs in the world:

One of the rarest marine formations on Earth, a double barrier reef. The Danajon Bank developed for about 6,000 years through coral growth. The two parallel reef systems stretch for about 130 kilometers across the Bohol Sea. The Danajon Bank is considered as a “coral cradle” where most of the marine species first evolved before spreading through various regions.

- Eskaya People:

They have a unique auxiliary language and writing system which is Eskayan. Conversations among the Eskaya primarily uses Boholano-Visayan dialect. Eskayan is very rarely spoken nowadays.

Fireflies:

Dense firefly clusters along rivers like Abatan are believed to represent ancestral spirits guiding fishermen, encouraging mangrove preservation.
